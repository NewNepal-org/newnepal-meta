## Case Summary

### Short Description (1-2 sentences)

Nepal's national flag carrier, Nepal Airlines Corporation (NAC), became
embroiled in a major corruption scandal over the 2017 purchase of two
Airbus A330-200 wide-body jets -- a deal worth about \$210 million that
investigators say was riddled with illegal procurement practices,
inflated pricing through intermediaries, and high-level abuse of
authority[\[1\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,Airbus%20A330%20jets%20by%20Nepal)[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).
The case has implicated top government officials and executives, leading
to parliamentary and anti-graft investigations and the conviction of
several key figures by a special court in
2024[\[3\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20court%20found%20several%20officials,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane)[\[4\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Former%20Tourism%20Minister%20Jeevan%20Bahadur,Shahi%2C%20however%2C%20was%20acquitted).

### Detailed Description

In 2016, Nepal Airlines Corporation (NAC) initiated a plan to acquire
two long-haul wide-body aircraft to expand its international fleet --
the largest aviation procurement in Nepal's history at an agreed cost of
\$209.6
million[\[1\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,Airbus%20A330%20jets%20by%20Nepal)[\[5\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,after%20adjusting).
Rather than purchasing brand-new planes directly from Airbus, the
state-run carrier controversially opted to buy two slightly used Airbus
A330-200 jets (each with around 1,000 flight hours) via a chain of
intermediary
companies[\[6\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=be%20more%20than%201%2C000%20flight,8%20million)[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by).
The U.S.-based supplier AAR Corp led a consortium that funneled the deal
through a special purpose vehicle called HiFly X Ireland Ltd, instead of
dealing directly with Airbus, a move that raised transparency concerns
and later prompted allegations of a deliberate scheme to inflate costs
and hide
kickbacks[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by)[\[8\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,in%20two%20jets%2C%20it%20said).
The aircraft were delivered in June and July 2018 and put into service
as "Annapurna" and "Makalu" in NAC's
fleet[\[9\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=At%20the%20time%2C%20then%20Tourism,procurement%2C%E2%80%9D%20the%20amended%20report%20reads)[\[10\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=The%20two%20wide,Photo%3A%20Courtesy%20of%20Nepal%20Airlines).

Soon after delivery, whistleblowers and lawmakers noticed irregularities
in the deal -- most glaringly, NAC had altered the technical
specifications to accept lower-performance jets without any price
reduction. The initial Request for Proposal had specified a maximum
takeoff weight (MTOW) of 242 tonnes per aircraft, but NAC ended up
accepting planes with only 230 tonnes
MTOW[\[11\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Specifications%20like%20maximum%20takeoff%20weight,4%20million%20in%20losses).
Investigators later calculated that this 12-tonne downgrade in
capability (affecting range and payload) should have come with a
significant discount; instead, NAC paid as if it were buying higher-spec
planes, causing an estimated loss of \$8.4
million[\[11\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Specifications%20like%20maximum%20takeoff%20weight,4%20million%20in%20losses).
Likewise, although the tender allowed "second-hand" jets (up to 1,000
flight hours, manufactured after 2014), NAC did not secure any
depreciation in price for the used condition -- in fact, the price was
escalated to the maximum, leading to another \~\$6.8 million in undue
costs[\[6\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=be%20more%20than%201%2C000%20flight,8%20million).
A breakdown by a parliamentary panel later showed multiple cost
inflation points: the purchase price was about \$23.6 million higher
than a benchmark price list suggested, plus the MTOW and used-plane
discrepancies, and even ancillary expenses like a 38-member junket to
inspect the aircraft in France added to the
losses[\[12\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,in%20two%20jets%2C%20it%20said)[\[13\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=before%20January%202014,losses%20to%20the%20national%20carrier).

Nepal's Parliament took note of mounting public criticism in late 2018
and assigned its Public Accounts Committee (PAC) to investigate. A PAC
subcommittee led by lawmaker Rajan KC conducted a deep-dive inquiry in
December 2018, reviewing procurement files and interviewing
officials[\[14\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,as%20NAC%20chairman%20during%20their)[\[15\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Minister%20Rabindra%20Adhikari%20and%20former,their%20tenure%20as%20tourism%20secretary).
In early January 2019, the subcommittee delivered a damning 58-page
report concluding that the wide-body procurement caused a **loss of NRs
4.35 billion** (about \$37 million) to the
state[\[16\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,committee%20report)[\[17\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,35%20billion%20to%20the%20government).
The report labeled NAC Managing Director **Sugat Ratna Kansakar** as the
*"kingpin"* of the scheme and found that successive Tourism Ministers
and Secretaries failed to stop, or even facilitated, the
irregularities[\[18\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=levels%20who%20were%20in%20charge,their%20tenure%20as%20tourism%20secretary).
It pointed out that the deal violated Nepal's Public Procurement Act and
NAC's own bylaws: by inviting bids for specific Airbus models and engine
brands (Rolls-Royce Trent 772B) and for used planes, NAC had effectively
tailor-made the tender for a particular supplier, flouting legal
provisions that forbid naming a particular brand or design in public
tenders to ensure fair
competition[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).
Moreover, NAC's internal rules require approaching aircraft
manufacturers for new planes, yet the board invoked a bylaw clause meant
for *"old"* aircraft to justify buying through a leasing agent -- a
loophole the PAC report described as an abuse of
process[\[19\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=As%20per%20Clause%20236,or%20airline%20operator%20besides%20manufacturers)[\[20\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=aircraft,or%20airline%20operator%20besides%20manufacturers).
The PAC suspected the creation of the Ireland-based shell company
**HiFly X** (by the Portuguese leasing firm HiFly and associates of AAR
Corp) was designed to obscure the money trail; the committee even
flagged Ireland as a tax haven and deemed HiFly X a "fictitious"
company, suggesting the possibility of **massive financial
irregularities and tax evasion** through profit
shifting[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by).
The PAC's findings and recommendations, endorsed on 2019-01-08 by the
full committee, urged the government and the Commission for
Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA) to take legal action against
dozens of individuals involved -- ranging from the sitting Tourism
Minister **Rabindra Adhikari** (for negligence in releasing final
payments) to former ministers **Jeevan Bahadur Shahi** and **Jitendra
Narayan Dev** (for approving initial payments), multiple former Tourism
Secretaries who chaired the NAC board (notably **Prem Kumar Rai**,
**Shankar Prasad Adhikari**, **Krishna Prasad Devkota**), and the entire
NAC leadership team
responsible[\[21\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=After%20the%20government%20announced%20in,body%20aircraft)[\[22\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=Likewise%2C%20the%20report%20has%20suggested,of%20authority%20and%20corruption).
PAC singled out **MD Kansakar** for immediate suspension and
accountability, given that he orchestrated the flawed procurement, and
indeed Kansakar resigned amid the
furor[\[23\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20PAC%20meeting%20also%20concluded,body%20procurement).

Following the PAC report, Nepal's anti-graft agency, the **CIAA**,
launched its own investigation into the wide-body deal. This probe took
several years, possibly delayed by the complexity and involvement of
powerful figures. Finally, on **2024-04-04**, the CIAA filed a
corruption charge-sheet at the Special Court in Kathmandu, naming **32
defendants (30 people and 2
companies)**[\[24\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=The%20Commission%20for%20Investigation%20of,body%20aircrafts)[\[25\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=Twenty,Regmi%2C%20spokesperson%20for%20the%20CIAA).
The accused included high-profile officials: former Tourism Minister
**Jeevan Bahadur Shahi**, former Tourism Secretaries **Sishir Kumar
Dhungana** and **Shankar Prasad Adhikari**, and former NAC Managing
Director **Sugat Ratna Kansakar** were charged as lead perpetrators of
an embezzlement estimated at NRs 1.478 billion (approximately \$13.4
million) during the aircraft
procurement[\[26\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=The%20anti,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s)[\[27\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=minister%20for%20Culture%2C%20Tourism%20and,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s).
In total, 24 Nepali individuals and 6 foreign nationals were indicted,
along with two corporate entities involved as
suppliers[\[28\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=aviation%20secretaries%20Sishir%20Kumar%20Dhungana,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s)[\[29\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=Twenty,Regmi%2C%20spokesperson%20for%20the%20CIAA).
Among the Nepalis were senior bureaucrats and NAC board members at the
time of purchase -- for example, Joint Secretary **Buddhi Sagar
Lamichhane** (who sat on NAC's board), former NAC Director **Ramesh
Bahadur Shah**, and ex-NAC board members **Tek Nath Acharya**, **Nima
Nuru Sherpa**, **Rishi Ram Pandey**, **Achyut Raj Pahadi**, **Jeevan
Prakash Sitaula**, etc., all accused of either colluding in bending the
rules or failing to prevent the
loss[\[29\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=Twenty,Regmi%2C%20spokesperson%20for%20the%20CIAA).
The CIAA notably decided *not* to charge two former tourism ministers
(Jitendra Dev and another ex-minister) due to "lack of
evidence"[\[30\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=However%2C%20the%20CIAA%20didn%E2%80%99t%20charge,evidence%20to%20prove%20their%20involvement)
-- a decision that later proved controversial. On the foreign side, the
charge-sheet highlighted the roles of executives linked to AAR Corp and
HiFly: it named (among others) British national **Deepak Sharma** (AAR
Corp's international supply chain head), American **John M. Holmes**
(CEO of AAR's aviation arm), Portuguese **Paulo Mirpuri** (President of
HiFly), and several German and British individuals tied to HiFly X or
its financier German Aviation Capital -- alleging that these foreign
agents conspired with Nepali officials to skim off money through
inflated pricing and
kickbacks[\[31\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=Similary%2C%20the%20CIAA%20has%20charged,Fulbright%20LLP%20Germany%2C%20and%20Ralf)[\[32\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=Similary%2C%20the%20CIAA%20has%20charged,Hifly%20X%20Ireland%20Limited%3B%20Markus).
The filing confirmed that **NAC's first Airbus A330 was delivered on
2018-06-28 and the second on 2018-07-26**, and that the deal's flaws
(like the reduced MTOW) were known soon after delivery, sparking public
outcry and
scrutiny[\[33\]\[34\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=The%20NAC%20acquired%20the%20first,procure%20those%20with%20242%20tons).
Notably, the CIAA concurred that procurement specifications had been
manipulated in violation of procurement laws -- for instance, by
specifying a particular engine make (Rolls-Royce) and using an agent
rather than dealing with the manufacturer, which the Public Procurement
Act forbids except under strict
conditions[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).

The case proceeded to trial at the Special Court (an anti-corruption
court). After months of hearings, on **2024-12-05** the Special Court
delivered a landmark verdict. It **convicted 10 individuals** --
including four key Nepali officials -- and upheld that roughly **NRs
1.47 billion** had been misappropriated in the wide-body
procurement[\[35\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20court%20found%20several%20officials,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane)[\[36\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20convicted%20individuals%20have%20been,half%20years%20in%20prison).
Those found guilty of corruption were: **Shankar Prasad Adhikari** (the
former Tourism Secretary who had acted as NAC's board chair during the
deal), **Sugat Ratna Kansakar** (ex-MD of NAC), **Sishir Kumar
Dhungana** (former Finance Secretary, earlier NAC board member), and
**Buddhi Sagar Lamichhane** (Joint Secretary and NAC board
member)[\[3\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20court%20found%20several%20officials,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane)[\[37\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=Among%20those%20found%20guilty%20were,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane).
Each of these officials was sentenced to prison (around 1.5 to 2.75
years, with Kansakar's term slightly reduced due to his age) and hefty
fines -- roughly Rs 122.6 million each, plus an equal amount in
compensation[\[36\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20convicted%20individuals%20have%20been,half%20years%20in%20prison)[\[38\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Kansakar%2C%20as%20a%20senior%20citizen%2C,reduction%20in%20his%20prison%20sentence).
The court also **convicted several foreign company representatives** who
had been charged in absentia -- they were handed sentences of about **1
year 6 months** in prison (also largely symbolic, since they were
abroad)[\[39\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Each%20convict%20must%20pay%20Rs,equal%20amount%20as%20a%20penalty)[\[40\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Foreign%20nationals%20involved%20in%20the,and%20six%20months%20in%20prison).
Importantly, the Special Court **acquitted 21 other accused persons**,
including former Tourism Minister **Jeevan Bahadur Shahi**, many
mid-level NAC staff, and even some high-ranking figures who had
originally been
implicated[\[41\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=In%20its%20earlier%20ruling%2C%20the,while%20it%20convicted%2011%20individuals)[\[37\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=Among%20those%20found%20guilty%20were,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane).
The judges concluded that while corruption did occur, responsibility lay
primarily with the top decision-makers who orchestrated and approved the
flawed procurement. The total confirmed loss -- NRs 1.47 billion -- was
lower than the PAC's earlier estimate, likely because the court focused
on clearly illegal payments and quantifiable kickbacks rather than all
notional
losses[\[42\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=A%20bench%20comprising%20the%20court%E2%80%99s,occurred%20in%20the%20procurement%20process)[\[43\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=minister%20for%20Culture%2C%20Tourism%20and,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s).
Still, Rs 1.47 billion (about \$12 million) is a huge sum in the Nepali
context, and the scandal has had lasting fallout.

Beyond the individual convictions, the Special Court's full written
verdict (made public in mid-2025) delivered an unexpected rebuke to the
CIAA itself. The court **criticized the anti-graft agency for "selective
prosecution,"** noting that some officials deeply involved in the
decision-making were left off the charge-sheet without clear
rationale[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[45\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=made%20public%20recently,%E2%80%9D).
In particular, it pointed out that **Prem Kumar Rai** -- the former
Tourism Secretary who had convened NAC's board and kick-started the
wide-body purchase in 2016, and who later (ironically) became the Chief
Commissioner of the CIAA -- was **not charged**, despite his early role
in forming the procurement
plan[\[46\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=Rai%2C%20who%20served%20as%20the,and%20executing%20the%20purchase%20process)[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust).
The verdict states that Rai chaired the crucial April 14, 2016 board
meeting that approved the wide-body project and formed the NAC
procurement subcommittee, effectively green-lighting the entire
process[\[46\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=Rai%2C%20who%20served%20as%20the,and%20executing%20the%20purchase%20process).
Yet when the CIAA prosecuted others who implemented that very decision,
Rai (by then heading the CIAA) **"excluded himself"**, which the court
said *undermines the principle of equal
justice*[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[47\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=excluding%20himself%20from%20prosecution%2C%20calling,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust).
The court also questioned why some NAC officials on the procurement
committee -- for example, **Yuvraj Adhikari**, an NAC board member
involved in negotiations with AAR -- were not indicted, whereas their
colleagues
were[\[48\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=impartiality%20and%20accountability%20within%20the,corruption%20body%20itself)[\[49\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20court%20ruled%20that%20excluding,%E2%80%9D).
These omissions, the judges implied, suggest potential bias or conflict
of interest within the CIAA's handling of the case. The Special Court's
stance has effectively kept the case alive: it implied that others could
still be held accountable in the future, and it opened the door for
further legal scrutiny of the investigators themselves for any abuse of
authority[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[50\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20verdict%20stated%2C%20%E2%80%9CThe%20decision,%E2%80%9D).

As of the latest updates, the saga is not fully over. The CIAA,
dissatisfied with the acquittals, has **appealed the Special Court's
judgment to the Supreme Court** (filed on 2025-12-02) seeking to convict
the remaining accused who were let
off[\[51\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=KATHMANDU%3A%20The%20Commission%20for%20the,body%20aircraft%20procurement%20case)[\[41\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=In%20its%20earlier%20ruling%2C%20the,while%20it%20convicted%2011%20individuals).
The convicted Nepali officials, for their part, may also appeal their
sentences. Meanwhile, the two Airbus jets at the center of the scandal
continue to fly for Nepal Airlines -- grim reminders of a deal gone
wrong. The case is widely seen as a test of Nepal's commitment to
accountability in public procurement. It exposed weaknesses in
oversight, the ease with which global suppliers and local actors could
collude to defraud the state, and the challenges of pursuing
high-profile corruption that spans multiple jurisdictions. Notably, in a
parallel development abroad, the U.S. Department of Justice investigated
AAR Corp (the American supplier) for Foreign Corrupt Practices Act
(FCPA) violations: **AAR admitted to paying bribes to Nepali officials**
through agents to secure the wide-body aircraft sale, and in late 2024
it agreed to pay about \$55 million in fines under a
settlement[\[52\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=KATHMANDU%2C%20Dec%2024%3A%20The%20AAR,SEC)[\[53\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=The%20AAR%20has%20admitted%20bribing,these%20officials%20through%20intermediary%20agents).
A former AAR executive, **Deepak Sharma**, pleaded guilty in U.S. court,
confessing that he conspired to bribe Nepali officials and even helped
draft NAC's tender requirements to favor AAR's
bid[\[54\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Who%20was%20the%20mastermind%20behind,bribing%20Nepali%20government%20officials)[\[55\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Bribery%20involving%20NAC%20wide,deal).
This external confirmation underscored that the NAC wide-body
procurement was not just a case of procedural lapse -- it was a
transnational bribery scheme that has now been at least partially
exposed. The scandal matters for Nepali citizens because it drained
millions from a state-owned company struggling to stay afloat, it
involved a blatant breach of rules by those entrusted to uphold them,
and its resolution (or lack thereof) will set a precedent for how boldly
Nepal can tackle grand corruption at the highest levels.

  before the scheme shifted through an Irish entity.

  significant as the place where the deal was orchestrated and later
  investigated under U.S. law (AAR's FCPA case). U.S. authorities found
  AAR executives conspired from Illinois to bribe Nepali
  officials[\[52\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=KATHMANDU%2C%20Dec%2024%3A%20The%20AAR,SEC)[\[53\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=The%20AAR%20has%20admitted%20bribing,these%20officials%20through%20intermediary%20agents).
- **Singhadurbar, Kathmandu** -- Nepal's administrative complex housing
  ministries. Key government decisions (e.g., ministry approvals,
  cabinet discussions if any) regarding the aircraft procurement and
  subsequent policy responses happened here.
- **Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu** -- The airport where
  the Airbus A330s (9N-ALY "Annapurna" and 9N-ALZ "Makalu") were
  delivered and based. The under-utilization of these jets (sometimes
  called "white elephants" in media) became a matter of public interest
  following the scandal.

## Key Allegations

1.  **Alteration of Specifications Post-Bidding:** It is alleged that
    NAC officials *unlawfully altered the aircraft specifications* after
    the tender to favor the supplier, without adjusting the price. The
    original bid called for Airbus A330-200s with **242 tonne MTOW**,
    but NAC accepted jets of **230 tonne MTOW** -- effectively
    lower-capacity planes -- while still paying full
    price[\[11\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Specifications%20like%20maximum%20takeoff%20weight,4%20million%20in%20losses).
    Investigators say this change, approved by NAC's leadership, caused
    a direct loss of about **\$8.4 million** to the airline, as the
    price should have been reduced for the downgraded
    specs[\[11\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Specifications%20like%20maximum%20takeoff%20weight,4%20million%20in%20losses).
    Those implicated include NAC's then-Managing Director and board, who
    knowingly approved the less capable aircraft for the same cost.

2.  **Use of Intermediaries and "Fictitious" Company to Inflate Cost:**
    Instead of procuring the aircraft directly from **Airbus (the
    manufacturer)**, NAC routed the purchase through a chain of
    intermediaries -- notably **AAR Corp (USA)** as the main contractor
    and an **offshore SPV "HiFly X Ireland Ltd"** set up by the
    suppliers[\[65\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Rs4)[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by).
    This indirect method is alleged to have *obscured pricing and
    enabled illicit mark-ups*. The Parliamentary investigation termed
    HiFly X a *"fictitious company"* and suspected it was created to
    siphon funds and possibly evade
    taxes[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by).
    The **Public Procurement Act** was allegedly breached, as it
    generally requires purchases from the manufacturer for new equipment
    and forbids specifying suppliers or unnecessary middlemen -- yet NAC
    officials justified the detour by misusing a clause meant for
    second-hand
    purchases[\[19\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=As%20per%20Clause%20236,or%20airline%20operator%20besides%20manufacturers)[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).
    The arrangement with intermediaries is alleged to have inflated the
    cost by millions, part of which officials then embezzled.

3.  **Tailoring of Tender and Violation of Procurement Laws:** NAC's
    Request for Proposal in 2016 **called for used Airbus A330s (\<=1000
    flight hours) with Rolls-Royce
    engines**[\[74\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=The%20NAC%20on%20September%2026,have%20clocked%201%2C000%20flights%20hours).
    Investigators allege this was deliberately tailored to match what
    AAR/HiFly had on offer, *violating Nepal's Public Procurement Act*.
    The Act prohibits specifying a particular brand or design in tender
    specs (to ensure open competition) except in rare
    cases[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).
    By naming *Airbus A330-200 with RR Trent 772B engines*, the NAC
    tender essentially excluded other options, flouting the
    law[\[2\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=kind%20of%20engines%20violated%20the,of%20procuring%20it%20directly%20from).
    Additionally, inviting bids for second-hand planes, when new ones
    could be bought from Airbus, went against procurement best practices
    and NAC's own rules (which prefer direct purchase from manufacturers
    for new
    aircraft)[\[19\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=As%20per%20Clause%20236,or%20airline%20operator%20besides%20manufacturers).
    These actions, approved by NAC's bid committee and board, are
    alleged to have been done to steer the contract to the chosen
    supplier consortium, undermining fair competition.

4.  **Financial Embezzlement and Kickbacks (\~Nrs 1.47 Billion):** The
    core allegation by prosecutors is that a significant portion of the
    payment for the jets was *misappropriated through corruption*. The
    Commission for Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA) quantified
    the **loss to the state at NRs 1.478 billion** ( \$13 million)
    during the
    purchase[\[28\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=aviation%20secretaries%20Sishir%20Kumar%20Dhungana,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s)[\[75\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=As%20per%20the%20CIAA%2C%20the,aircraft%20by%20Nepal%20Airlines%20Corporation).
    This suggests that the deal was overpriced and the excess was shared
    as bribes and illegal commissions among the accused Nepali officials
    and foreign agents. Indeed, evidence later emerged (via a U.S.
    Justice Department investigation) that **AAR Corp paid at least
    \$2.5 million in bribes** to Nepali officials to secure this
    deal[\[76\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=The%20Illinois,issued%20by%20the%20AAR%20Corp)[\[54\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Who%20was%20the%20mastermind%20behind,bribing%20Nepali%20government%20officials).
    Emails and testimony from an AAR executive showed collusion: the
    draft tender specifications were allegedly leaked and tweaked with
    supplier input, and shell agents were used to funnel
    payments[\[54\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Who%20was%20the%20mastermind%20behind,bribing%20Nepali%20government%20officials)[\[55\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Bribery%20involving%20NAC%20wide,deal).
    Key NAC officials -- including the managing director, board members,
    and ministry officials -- are accused of abusing their authority to
    approve an unlawfully expensive contract in exchange for personal
    gain. (All accused have denied wrongdoing, but multiple executives
    were convicted on corruption charges by the Special Court in 2024,
    confirming elements of this allegation.)

5.  **Breach of Public Trust and Abuse of Authority:** (Related to the
    above) It is alleged that officials at the highest level -- from
    ministers to secretaries -- *abused their public positions* to push
    through the deal despite red flags. For instance, Tourism Minister
    **Rabindra Adhikari** was accused by a parliamentary panel of
    rushing the final payment in 2018 even after audit warnings, thus
    "neglecting his
    duty"[\[77\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20subcommittee%20in%20its%20report,tranche%20of%20the%20purchase%20price).
    Similarly, Secretaries **Prem Kumar Rai** and **Shankar P.
    Adhikari** were accused of bending rules (forming committees,
    approving tenders) that enabled the
    scam[\[59\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=NAC%27s%20management%2C%20find%20a%20strategic,body%20aircraft)[\[22\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=Likewise%2C%20the%20report%20has%20suggested,of%20authority%20and%20corruption).
    The subsequent inquiry also alleged **selective accountability** --
    that the anti-corruption chief (Rai) did not recuse himself despite
    his prior involvement, leading to suspicions of a cover-up for
    certain individuals. This allegation came to light in the Special
    Court's remarks that not charging some equally-responsible officials
    demonstrated bias and bad
    intent[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[50\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20verdict%20stated%2C%20%E2%80%9CThe%20decision,%E2%80%9D).
    In essence, the case alleges a **systemic governance failure**,
    where checks and balances were subverted by those in power for
    personal or political benefit.

*(Note: All allegations above were made in investigation reports or
charge-sheets. Some have been proven in court (e.g., the Special Court
upheld the charge of financial loss due to spec changes and
intermediaries), while others remain allegations pending appeal or
further investigation.)*

- **Title**: Government Provides Financial Guarantee
- **Description**: Nepal's Cabinet (or Ministry of Finance) issues a

### Event 4

- **Date**: **2016-09-26**
- **Title**: NAC Issues Tender for Two Airbus A330-200s (Used)
- **Description**: NAC publishes a global **Request for Proposal (RFP)**

  jets[\[74\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=The%20NAC%20on%20September%2026,have%20clocked%201%2C000%20flights%20hours).
  Notably, the tender specifies the aircraft must be **Airbus A330-200
  with Rolls-Royce Trent 772B engines**, with no more than 1,000 flight

  intermediaries rather than new ones from the

### Event 5

- **Date**: **2017-04-07** *(approx)*
- **Title**: Contract Signed with AAR Corp for \$209.6 Million
- **Description**: After evaluating bids, NAC signs a purchase agreement
  in **April 2017** with **AAR Corp**, a U.S.-based aviation supplier,
  to acquire two Airbus A330-200 at a total price of **USD \$209.6
  million**[\[5\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,after%20adjusting).
  This is reported to be the largest deal in Nepal's aviation
  history[\[1\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,Airbus%20A330%20jets%20by%20Nepal).
  AAR Corp isn't an aircraft manufacturer but leads a consortium
  including **HiFly (Portugal)**. Under the deal structure, AAR/HiFly
  will deliver the jets via a newly created Irish intermediary (HiFly
  X). The agreed price, roughly \$104.8M per plane, appears higher than
  expected for slightly used jets -- NAC's internal team had earlier
  estimated about \$93M
  each[\[8\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,in%20two%20jets%2C%20it%20said).
  NAC pays a **commitment fee**/down payment to HiFly by mid-2017
  (approved by Tourism Minister J. B.
  Shahi)[\[82\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=had%20said%20he%20was%20not,the%20NAC%20jet%20procurement%20process).
  Around this time, questions are raised internally about whether Airbus
  was approached directly and what discounts were obtained, but NAC
  officials do not provide the "bill of sale" to clarify
  pricing[\[83\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=had%20carried%20out%20only%20a,house%20investigation).

### Event 6

- **Date**: **2018-06-28**
- **Title**: First A330 ("Annapurna") Delivered to NAC
- **Description**: Nepal Airlines takes delivery of the **first Airbus
  A330-200** in Toulouse, France, which flies to Kathmandu on June
  28, 2018. The aircraft (9N-ALY, later named "Annapurna") is greeted in
  Nepal as a milestone for the national carrier. A month later, on
  **2018-07-26**, the **second A330** (9N-ALZ "Makalu") is
  delivered[\[84\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=Image)[\[10\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=The%20two%20wide,Photo%3A%20Courtesy%20of%20Nepal%20Airlines).
  With this, NAC doubles its wide-body fleet. However, insiders note
  discrepancies: the delivered jets have **230-tonne MTOW**
  capabilities, whereas NAC's initial plan was for 242-tonne
  versions[\[33\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=The%20NAC%20acquired%20the%20first,procure%20those%20with%20242%20tons).
  This difference comes to light publicly a few months later, raising
  suspicions that NAC **overpaid for lower-spec planes**. By late 2018,
  NAC struggles to deploy the jets optimally (routes and load factors
  aren't as projected), leading to media scrutiny over whether the
  purchase was a financially prudent decision.

### Event 7

- **Date**: **2018-12-19** *(approx)*
- **Title**: Parliament's PAC Opens Inquiry into Wide-Body Deal
- **Description**: Amid growing controversy in the press and questions
  in Parliament about the wide-body purchase, the **Public Accounts
  Committee (PAC)** of the House summons officials and launches an
  investigation. PAC forms a **subcommittee (investigative panel)**
  chaired by MP **Rajan KC** to probe alleged irregularities. Over \~20
  days, the PAC subcommittee reviews documents and takes statements from
  key figures (including current and former tourism ministers and NAC
  officials)[\[85\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,Krishna%20Prasad%20Devkota%2C%20and%20former)[\[82\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=had%20said%20he%20was%20not,the%20NAC%20jet%20procurement%20process).
  They also consult experts on aircraft pricing. Notably, by this time,
  a *leak* revealed NAC had paid in full without clarity on discounts,
  and questions were raised why a little-known Irish firm was involved.
  The inquiry has bipartisan support due to the scale of the deal.

### Event 8

- **Date**: **2019-01-02**
- **Title**: PAC Subcommittee Report Finds Rs 4.35 Billion Loss
- **Description**: The PAC's subcommittee submits its detailed
  **investigation report** on the NAC wide-body procurement. In a PAC
  meeting on January 2, 2019 (made public on Jan 3), the panel concludes
  that the deal **cost the government NRs 4.35 billion in losses**
  through overpricing and procedural
  abuse[\[16\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,committee%20report)[\[17\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20%24209.6,35%20billion%20to%20the%20government).
  The report names over **30 individuals** for action. It castigates
  NAC's Managing Director **Sugat Kansakar** as the *"main culprit"* and
  recommends his immediate
  suspension[\[18\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=levels%20who%20were%20in%20charge,their%20tenure%20as%20tourism%20secretary)[\[23\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20PAC%20meeting%20also%20concluded,body%20procurement).
  It also holds **Tourism Minister Rabindra Adhikari** responsible for
  not halting payment and **former Ministers J. B. Shahi and J. N. Dev**
  for initiating payments, saying all three should be held *"morally and
  legally
  accountable"*[\[58\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20full%20committee%20meeting%20held,recommended%20%27maximum%20punishment%27%20for%20him)[\[86\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20sub,and%20former%20tourism%20ministers).
  Two sitting Secretaries -- **Prem K. Rai** and **Krishna P. Devkota**
  -- who chaired NAC at different stages, and former Secy **Shankar P.
  Adhikari**, are found to have abused their authority and are singled
  out for legal
  action[\[18\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=levels%20who%20were%20in%20charge,their%20tenure%20as%20tourism%20secretary)[\[22\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=Likewise%2C%20the%20report%20has%20suggested,of%20authority%20and%20corruption).
  The report itemizes the financial irregularities (price inflation,
  MTOW downgrade, lack of depreciation for used planes, unnecessary
  expenditures)[\[12\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=The%20sub,in%20two%20jets%2C%20it%20said)[\[87\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=Specifications%20like%20maximum%20takeoff%20weight,4%20million%20in%20losses).
  It questions the creation of HiFly X in Ireland and suggests money
  might have been siphoned through that
  SPV[\[7\]](https://kathmandupost.com/valley/2019/01/03/wide-body-jet-deal-cost-carrier-rs-435b-in-losses-kc-report#:~:text=forming%20a%20special%20purpose%20vehicle%E2%80%94Hi,in%20the%20world%20used%20by).
  PAC forwards the report to the government and **CIAA**, and on
  **2019-01-07** the full PAC Committee endorses it, formally
  **recommending the government and CIAA to take swift legal action**
  against all implicated officials and to suspend those still in
  office[\[88\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=PAC%20has%20now%20recommended%20the,as%20per%20the%20legal%20provisions)[\[23\]](https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/pac-for-action-against-current-and-ex-ministers-clean-chit-for-rai#:~:text=The%20PAC%20meeting%20also%20concluded,body%20procurement).
  (Following this, MD Kansakar resigns under pressure, and the
  government says it is cooperating with the investigation.)

### Event 9

- **Date**: **2019-02** to **2020**
- **Title**: Further Inquiries and International Developments
- **Description**: The CIAA begins examining PAC's evidence. A parallel
  investigation by the **Office of Auditor General** and other bodies is
  also conducted into NAC's financial management. During 2019, Nepal's
  aviation ministry is rocked by tragedy when Minister Rabindra Adhikari
  dies in a helicopter crash (Feb 2019), which stalls some follow-up.
  Meanwhile, in **2019-2020, U.S. authorities (DOJ/SEC)** investigate
  AAR Corp under the FCPA. AAR self-discloses potential bribery in the
  Nepal deal, and a former AAR executive **Deepak Sharma** (a UK citizen
  of Nepali origin) is identified as orchestrating bribes to NAC and
  ministry officials starting in
  2015[\[76\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=The%20Illinois,issued%20by%20the%20AAR%20Corp)[\[54\]](https://www.myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/us-firm-fined-over-55-million-for-bribing-nepali-officials-to-secure-nac-wi...-676aca1717a0e.html#:~:text=Who%20was%20the%20mastermind%20behind,bribing%20Nepali%20government%20officials).
  This leads to a confidential U.S. plea by Sharma in 2020 (publicly
  revealed later) and AAR's negotiations with DOJ. These developments
  are not widely known in Nepal yet, but presumably CIAA coordinates
  informally with foreign law enforcement for evidence.

### Event 10

- **Date**: **2024-04-04**
- **Title**: CIAA Files Corruption Case at Special Court
- **Description**: After five years of investigation, Nepal's CIAA
  formally **files a charge-sheet** against **32 defendants** at the
  Special Court,
  Kathmandu[\[24\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=The%20Commission%20for%20Investigation%20of,body%20aircrafts)[\[89\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=minister%20for%20Culture%2C%20Tourism%20and,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s).
  The filing (Special Court Case No. 1B/2079) accuses the group of
  **corruption and abuse of authority** in the wide-body procurement,
  citing a loss of **NRs 1.4785 billion** to the
  state[\[28\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=aviation%20secretaries%20Sishir%20Kumar%20Dhungana,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s)[\[75\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=As%20per%20the%20CIAA%2C%20the,aircraft%20by%20Nepal%20Airlines%20Corporation).
  The defendants include 24 Nepali individuals -- such as ex-Minister
  **Jeevan B. Shahi**, ex-Secretaries **Shankar P. Adhikari** and
  **Sishir K. Dhungana**, former NAC MD **Sugat Kansakar**, NAC board
  members and directors, ministry officials -- and 6 foreign nationals
  plus 2 companies (the supplier
  firms)[\[26\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=The%20anti,procurement%20of%20two%20Airbus%20A330s)[\[25\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=Twenty,Regmi%2C%20spokesperson%20for%20the%20CIAA).
  CIAA's complaint outlines how NAC's RFP violated the Procurement Act
  by specifying a certain aircraft/engine and by procuring via an agent,
  and how the accused officials willfully allowed an unnecessary
  middleman (HiFly X) which inflated
  costs[\[90\]](https://en.setopati.com/political/162857#:~:text=The%20sub,it%20directly%20from%20the%20manufacturer).
  It alleges the decision-makers colluded with AAR/HiFly to award the
  contract on unfavorable terms, with parts of the payments being
  misused as kickbacks. Notably, the CIAA drops charges against a couple
  of political figures named by PAC (e.g., former Minister Jitendra Dev)
  due to "insufficient
  evidence"[\[30\]](https://kathmandupost.com/national/2024/04/04/ciaa-filed-graft-case-against-32-including-former-minister-and-ex-secretaries-in-wide-body-aircraft-deal#:~:text=However%2C%20the%20CIAA%20didn%E2%80%99t%20charge,evidence%20to%20prove%20their%20involvement).
  The case is registered and proceeds to trial with **Special Court
  hearings starting mid-2024**.

### Event 11

- **Date**: **2024-12-05**
- **Title**: Special Court Verdict -- Key Convictions and Acquittals
- **Description**: The Special Court of Nepal delivers its **verdict**
  on the wide-body corruption case. In a mixed outcome, the court
  **convicts 10 individuals and clears 21 others** of the
  charges[\[41\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=In%20its%20earlier%20ruling%2C%20the,while%20it%20convicted%2011%20individuals)[\[37\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=Among%20those%20found%20guilty%20were,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane).
  Convicted are the four principal Nepali officials -- **Sugat R.
  Kansakar, Shankar P. Adhikari, Sishir K. Dhungana, Buddhi S.
  Lamichhane** -- as well as a handful of foreign company
  representatives who were tried in
  absentia[\[3\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20court%20found%20several%20officials,Joint%20Secretary%20Buddhi%20Sagar%20Lamichhane)[\[40\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Foreign%20nationals%20involved%20in%20the,and%20six%20months%20in%20prison).
  The court finds that corruption *did* occur, affirming that **NRs 1.47
  billion was misappropriated** in the
  deal[\[42\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=A%20bench%20comprising%20the%20court%E2%80%99s,occurred%20in%20the%20procurement%20process).
  However, it judges that many lower-level accused either had no
  decision-making role or insufficient evidence against them, resulting
  in the acquittal of, among others, former Minister **Jeevan B. Shahi**
  (who had been the highest-profile
  defendant)[\[4\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Former%20Tourism%20Minister%20Jeevan%20Bahadur,Shahi%2C%20however%2C%20was%20acquitted).
  The convicted Nepali officials receive prison sentences ranging from
  **1.5 years to 2 years 9 months**, plus heavy fines and confiscation
  equal to the amount embezzled (approximately Rs 122.6 million fine +
  Rs 122.6 million compensation
  each)[\[36\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=The%20convicted%20individuals%20have%20been,half%20years%20in%20prison)[\[39\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Each%20convict%20must%20pay%20Rs,equal%20amount%20as%20a%20penalty).
  Because Kansakar is over 65, his sentence is halved per the Senior
  Citizens
  Act[\[91\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=a%20fine%20and%20restitution%20of,25%20million%20each).
  The convicted foreign agents are sentenced to **1 year 6 months in
  jail** (though it is largely symbolic unless they are
  extradited)[\[39\]](https://newbusinessage.com/news/41538/special-court-convicts-top-nepali-officials-in-wide-body-scam/#:~:text=Each%20convict%20must%20pay%20Rs,equal%20amount%20as%20a%20penalty).
  In its judgment, the Special Court notably criticizes the CIAA for
  *not* indicting some individuals who were clearly involved in the
  initial decision -- highlighting that **Prem K. Rai** (the CIAA Chief,
  ex-Secretary) and **Yuvraj Adhikari** (NAC's Chair) were spared
  despite their
  roles[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[48\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=impartiality%20and%20accountability%20within%20the,corruption%20body%20itself).
  The court terms this a *"selective prosecution"* and suggests it
  reflected bias, which undermines public
  trust[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust).
  Nevertheless, the verdict is hailed as a rare instance of senior
  officials being held accountable for a major corruption scandal in
  Nepal. Following the verdict, 10 convicted Nepalis (or those present)
  are taken into custody to serve their sentences, and attention turns
  to recovering the fines/amounts from them.

### Event 12

- **Date**: **2025-07-24**
- **Title**: Special Court's Full Verdict Published -- Rebuke to CIAA
- **Description**: The Special Court releases its **full written
  judgment**, which becomes public on this date (sparking media
  analysis)[\[92\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=aircraft%20corruption%20case%2C%20calling%20it,%E2%80%9D).
  The 300-page verdict provides detailed reasoning. It reiterates how
  the procurement process began on 2016-04-14 with a board decision by
  Prem K. Rai and that the process followed formal steps but with
  mal-intent behind the
  scenes[\[79\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=According%20to%20the%20verdict%2C%20the,b)[\[93\]](https://www.newbusinessage.com/news/44783/special-court-flags-ciaas-selective-prosecution-in-wide-body-corruption-case/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CIt%20seems%20the%20charge%20sheet,been%20prosecuted%2C%E2%80%9D%20the%20verdict%20states).
  Crucially, the verdict openly questions why the CIAA did not charge
  certain key officials. It names CIAA Chief **Prem Kumar Rai** as
  having led the procurement's launch yet facing no charges -- calling
  this omission a breach of "equal justice"
  principles[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust).
  It also mentions NAC's Executive Chair **Yuvraj Adhikari** as an
  example of someone involved in negotiations but not
  prosecuted[\[48\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=impartiality%20and%20accountability%20within%20the,corruption%20body%20itself).
  The court suggests that such exclusions indicate either **negligence
  or protection**, and warns that this selective approach violates
  constitutional
  rights[\[44\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20Special%20Court%20criticized%20Rai,legal%20fairness%20and%20public%20trust)[\[50\]](https://english.clickmandu.com/2025/07/608/#:~:text=The%20verdict%20stated%2C%20%E2%80%9CThe%20decision,%E2%80%9D).
  The verdict's publication fuels public debate and puts pressure on the
  CIAA to explain its decisions. It also implies that those not charged
  (like Rai) could potentially be investigated if new complaints are
  lodged, because the court stopped short of exonerating them, instead
  highlighting the lapse in prosecution.

### Event 13

- **Date**: **2025-12-02**
- **Title**: CIAA Appeals to Supreme Court Against Acquittals
- **Description**: Unhappy with the Special Court verdict that acquitted
  the majority of accused, the **CIAA files an appeal** at the Supreme
  Court of
  Nepal[\[51\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=KATHMANDU%3A%20The%20Commission%20for%20the,body%20aircraft%20procurement%20case).
  In its appeal, the CIAA argues that the Special Court erred in letting
  off many defendants and that all 32 originally charged should be
  punished as per the evidence. CIAA points to what it views as
  misinterpretation by the lower court, especially regarding the
  involvement of political superiors. The agency essentially asks the
  Supreme Court to **overturn the acquittals of 21 individuals** and
  confirm that the corruption was a broader
  conspiracy[\[94\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=CIAA%20spokesperson%20Rajendra%20Kumar%20Paudel,in%20the%20controversial%20procurement%20deal)[\[95\]](https://english.khabarhub.com/2025/02/509912/#:~:text=Calling%20the%20verdict%20flawed%2C%20the,judgment%20on%20December%205%2C%202024).
  This move is significant -- it's rare for the CIAA to challenge a
  court decision, and it indicates the case's high stakes.
  Simultaneously, some of the convicted parties (e.g., Kansakar) also
  appeal their convictions, claiming innocence. As of the end of 2025,
  the Supreme Court has admitted the case, and proceedings are ongoing.
  The final judgment (which could exonerate or implicate additional
  figures) is eagerly awaited and will likely set a precedent for
  accountability in large-scale procurements.

*(Events continue to unfold -- e.g., any Supreme Court decision in 2026
or further action against uncharged individuals would be subsequent
events.)*
