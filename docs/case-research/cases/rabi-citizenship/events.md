## Timeline

Document all significant events in chronological order. Be as detailed as possible for each event, including specific actions taken, people involved, amounts of money (if applicable), and the significance of each development.

### Event 1
- **Date**: 2019-01-01 (2077-12-25) ✅
- **Title**: Rabi Lamichhane joins Gorkha Media Network
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane quits News24 Nepal and joins Gorkha Media Network Pvt. Ltd. as its Managing Director. He is also awarded 180,000 'sweat shares.' At this time, Lamichhane was a media personality and journalist, not yet involved in politics.

### Event 2
- **Date**: 2021-04-18 (2078-01-05)
- **Title**: Direct Transfer to Lamichhane's Account
- **Description**: Supreme Savings and Credit Cooperative (Butwal) makes a large irregular payment of Rs 20 million directly to Rabi Lamichhane's personal Global IME Bank account. The deposit was made via check in Lamichhane's name, executed by cooperative employee Roshni Gurung at the instruction of manager Sagar KC. This transaction is later identified as part of the scheme to siphon depositor funds to Gorkha Media Network.

### Event 3
- **Date**: 2021-06-01
- **Title**: Peak Period of Fund Diversions
- **Description**: Investigation reveals that between mid-2021 and 2022, a total of Rs 650 million from five cooperatives (Surya Darshan, Supreme, Sahara, Swarnalaxmi, and Sano Paila) were diverted into Gorkha Media Network's accounts. Lamichhane, as Managing Director holding 15% "sweat equity" stake, signed audit reports treating these inflows as legitimate loans or advances despite the company having no other revenue stream.

### Event 4
- **Date**: 2022-06-15/16 (2079 03 01/2) ✅
- **Title**: Lamichhane Leaves Gorkha Media
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane sells his shares to G.B. Rai for a total of N.Rs. 1 crore 80 lakhs.

### Event 4A
- **Date**: 2022-07-01 (2079-03-17) ✅
- **Title**: Rabi Lamichhane Establishes Rastriya Swatantra Party
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane establishes the Rastriya Swatantra Party (RSP) and becomes its founding Chairman.

### Event 4B
- **Date**: 2022-12-22 (2079-09-07) ✅
- **Title**: Rabi Lamichhane Becomes Member of Parliament
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane is elected as Member of Parliament to the Pratinidhi Sabha, securing 49,300 votes from Chitwan B constituency.

### Event 4C
- **Date**: 2022-12-26 (2079-09-11) ✅
- **Title**: Rabi Lamichhane Becomes Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane is appointed as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs in the coalition government.

### Event 5
- **Date**: 2023-01-27 (2079-10-13) ✅
- **Title**: Lamichhane Loses Deputy PM Position (First Term)
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane loses his position as Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister after the Supreme Court rules on his citizenship issue. He is forced to resign from Parliament and his ministerial positions, though he later regains his citizenship and returns to politics.

### Event 6
- **Date**: 2023-01-29 (2079-10-15) ✅
- **Title**: Lamichhane Continues as RSP Chairman
- **Description**: Despite losing his government positions, Rabi Lamichhane continues as Chairman of the Rastriya Swatantra Party, maintaining his political influence and party leadership during his temporary absence from formal government roles.

### Event 7
- **Date**: 2023-04-16
- **Title**: First Legal Case Filed
- **Description**: The Supreme Cooperative fraud case is first registered at Rupandehi District Court with claims of Rs 841 million against multiple defendants including Lamichhane. Over 10,000 depositors were affected by the Supreme Cooperative collapse, which had misappropriated more than Rs 1.12 billion of savings. Cooperative chairman Om Prakash Gurung is arrested and remains in custody.

### Event 8
- **Date**: 2023-04-28 (2080-01-15) ✅
- **Title**: Lamichhane Returns as Member of Parliament
- **Description**: Rabi Lamichhane returns as Member of Parliament after resolving his citizenship issues, regaining his seat in the Pratinidhi Sabha and resuming his role as a key opposition figure.

### Event 9
- **Date**: 2023-08-04
- **Title**: Government-Victim MOU Signed
- **Description**: After months of protests by cooperative victims in Kathmandu, the government signs a 7-point Memorandum of Understanding with protest leaders. The MOU promises relief measures including establishing a deposit security fund, setting up a credit information center and special tribunal for cooperative disputes, and pursuing legal reforms. This temporarily calmed protests but increased pressure on political leaders to address high-profile scams.

### Event 7
- **Date**: 2024-05-28
- **Title**: Parliamentary Special Inquiry Committee Formed
- **Description**: The Parliamentary Special Inquiry Committee on cooperative fraud is formed by the House of Representatives after intense political pressure from opposition parties. The seven-member multi-party committee, chaired by UML lawmaker Surya Thapa, is tasked with investigating crisis-ridden cooperatives, specifically including those linked to funding Gorkha Media Network. The committee is given 3 months to report with a mandate to trace cooperative funds, identify responsible individuals, and recommend legal action.

### Event 8
- **Date**: 2024-09-16
- **Title**: Parliamentary Committee Report Submitted
- **Description**: The parliamentary inquiry committee tables its report in Parliament, concluding that Rs 650 million from five cooperatives was illegally diverted to Gorkha Media Network. The report finds that while direct evidence of Lamichhane's personal role in authorizing transfers wasn't obtained, he was the managing director and shareholder who benefited from and utilized misappropriated funds. The committee also finds forgery and fraud in handling of Gorkha Media's shares, with Lamichhane's claimed sale based on false documents. The report recommends prosecuting four individuals: Lamichhane, GB Rai, Chhabi Lal Joshi, and Kumar Ramtel.

### Event 9
- **Date**: 2024-10-18
- **Title**: Lamichhane Arrested
- **Description**: Acting on the committee's findings, Nepal Police's Central Investigation Bureau arrests Rabi Lamichhane from the RSP party office in Kathmandu on charges of organized crime and misappropriation of cooperative funds. The arrest catches media and public attention as a dramatic fall for the sitting MP and former minister. Lamichhane is taken first to Pokhara for legal proceedings related to the Surya Darshan Cooperative case.

### Event 10
- **Date**: 2024-12-22
- **Title**: Formal Charge-Sheet Filed in Kaski
- **Description**: District Police in Kaski formally file a charge-sheet against Rabi Lamichhane and 43 others in the Surya Darshan Cooperative case. The Kaski District Government Attorney's Office registers cases of cooperative fraud, organized crime, and money laundering, seeking a 15-year prison sentence for Lamichhane along with confiscation of Rs 278.9 million. The charges specify that Lamichhane transferred depositors' funds from Surya Darshan Cooperative into his personal bank account and Gorkha Media's account while serving as managing director.

### Event 11
- **Date**: 2025-01-09
- **Title**: First Bail Granted in Kaski
- **Description**: The Kaski District Court grants Lamichhane bail of Rs 6.5 million in the Surya Darshan cooperative fraud case. Judge Nitij Jung Rana orders his release after a week-long closed hearing, though Lamichhane cannot walk free yet as he faces cases in other districts. This begins a series of bail hearings across multiple jurisdictions.

### Event 12
- **Date**: 2025-04-04
- **Title**: High Court Revokes Bail
- **Description**: The Butwal Bench of Tulsipur High Court intervenes and revokes the Rupandehi District Court's bail order in the Supreme Cooperative case. The High Court decides that Lamichhane should remain in pre-trial detention, deeming the charges serious. This overturns the earlier Rs 10 million bail he had posted, meaning Lamichhane is re-arrested and jailed despite prior releases. He is held at Bhairahawa Prison and later transferred to Nakkhu Jail in Lalitpur.

### Event 13
- **Date**: 2025-05-13
- **Title**: Parsa Case Filed - Largest Embezzlement Revealed
- **Description**: The Parsa District Government Attorney's Office files a charge-sheet against Rabi Lamichhane and 28 others in the Sano Paila Cooperative fraud case. This case reveals the largest scale of embezzlement: police allege Rs 2.3 billion was swindled from 5,873 depositors of Sano Paila Cooperative, with Rs 115.6 million directly tied to Lamichhane's involvement. The charges show Rs 100.7 million from Sano Paila was invested into Gorkha Media Network when Lamichhane was MD.

### Event 14
- **Date**: 2025-05-23
- **Title**: Supreme Court Upholds Detention
- **Description**: A division bench of Supreme Court Justices Nahakul Subedi and Balkrishna Dhakal quashes Lamichhane's habeas corpus petition filed by his wife. The Supreme Court upholds the High Court's decision, stating there is "no reasonable basis to believe the defendant is innocent" based on available evidence. This ruling solidifies the legitimacy of his detention under the Organized Crime Act.

### Event 15
- **Date**: 2025-12-18
- **Title**: Historic Bail Order - Release on Rs 268 Million Bond
- **Description**: In a landmark decision, the Butwal High Court orders Lamichhane's release on bail totaling Rs 268 million plus Rs 100 million additional deposit in the Supreme Cooperative case. A division bench of Chief Judge Basudev Acharya and Judge Tej Narayan Paudel rules that pre-trial detention is not necessary if the accused is cooperating and securing the claimed sum. The historic bail amount reflects the massive financial liability established by forensic audit while balancing presumption of innocence. After 14 months in custody, Lamichhane is set to be released once procedural formalities are completed.
