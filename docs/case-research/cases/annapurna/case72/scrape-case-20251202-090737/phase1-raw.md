Here are the comprehensive raw findings regarding the case of Madan Prasad Gupta.

### Case Summary
Madan Prasad Gupta, a non-gazetted first-class officer (Nayab Subba), has been implicated in multiple corruption cases, primarily focusing on the embezzlement of revenue and the illegal acquisition of disproportionate assets during his tenure at various Land Revenue Offices in Nepal. The Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA) filed a major case against him in 2076 BS (2019 AD) for illegally amassing wealth amounting to over 14 crore rupees. He has since been involved in other corruption charges and was sentenced by the Special Court in January 2024.

### Timeline of Events

*   **2047 BS (c. 1990 AD):** Madan Prasad Gupta began his government service as a Kharidar (clerk) at the Land Revenue Office, Mustang.
*   **2064 BS (c. 2007 AD):** Promoted to Nayab Subba (Naib Subba) and transferred to the Land Revenue Office, Birgunj, Parsa.
*   **2064 - 2074 BS (c. 2007 - 2017 AD):** Served at the Land Revenue Office in Parsa, a period during which significant embezzlement allegedly occurred.
*   **January 29, 2013 (and for eight days following):** Allegedly embezzled Rs 162,000 out of Rs 1.4 million in collected revenue at the Land Revenue Office in Parsa.
*   **Fiscal Year 2072/73 BS (c. 2015-2016 AD):** The CIAA filed a case against Gupta and other employees for embezzling revenue at the Land Revenue Office, Parsa. The amount attributed to Gupta in this case was Rs 9.58 million.
*   **Mangsir 12, 2076 BS (c. November 28, 2019 AD):** The CIAA filed a major corruption case against Madan Prasad Gupta at the Special Court for illegal acquisition of property.
*   **April 12, 2023:** The CIAA filed a corruption case against Gupta and three other former employees of the Land Revenue Office, Parsa, including the office chief.
*   **September 23, 2023 (approximate date):** The CIAA filed its most recent case against Gupta at the time, accusing him of embezzling approximately Rs 12.5 million while at the Land Revenue Office in Parsa.
*   **January 23, 2024:** The Special Court sentenced Madan Prasad Gupta to five years in prison for his involvement in seven corruption cases. His co-accused, accountant Nagendra Prasad Chaudhary, was sentenced to four years.

### Allegations and Financial Discrepancies

**1. Illegal Asset Accumulation Case (Filed in 2076 BS):**
*   **Total Illegally Acquired Assets:** The CIAA claims Gupta illegally amassed **NPR 14,81,00,000** (Fourteen Crore Eighty-One Lakh Rupees). *Note: The initial document states 14 crore 89 lakh 23 rupees, a slight discrepancy.*
*   **Total Assets (Illegal and Legal):** Gupta's total movable and immovable property was valued at **NPR 19,36,00,000** (Nineteen Crore Thirty-Six Lakh Rupees) during his 29-year service period.
*   **Legitimate Income:** His legal income from salary and other valid sources was calculated to be only **NPR 4,54,00,000** (Four Crore Fifty-Four Lakh Rupees).
*   **Method of Corruption:** Gupta allegedly misused his position by:
    *   Collecting official revenue from service seekers but depositing only a portion of it, keeping the rest for himself.
    *   Taking large sums of money to settle disputes over public and controversial land in his favor.
    *   Exceeding his authority by handling work from registration departments he was not assigned to at the Land Revenue Offices in Kalaiya and Simra.
*   **Concealment of Assets:** Gupta is accused of hiding his illegally earned property in the names of his wife, Rita Devi, and sons, Vicky and Rocky Gupta, leading to charges against them as well. The assets of Kavita Kumari, Niraj Kumar Gupta, Rajesh Kumar Gupta, and two other LRO employees (Juguli Prasad and Jagatnarayan Yadav) were also brought under investigation.

**2. Revenue Embezzlement Cases (LRO, Parsa):**
*   Gupta was found guilty in seven separate corruption cases related to revenue embezzlement.
*   In a case from FY 2072/73 BS, he was accused of misappropriating **NPR 9.58 million**. Another report from January 2024 states the CIAA sought a fine of **NPR 9,508,000** for this embezzlement.
*   The method involved not reporting the full amount of revenue collected during land transactions to the accounting branch and creating two sets of records to hide the fraud.
*   Another case involved embezzling approximately **NPR 12.5 million** at the LRO, Parsa.

### Legal Status and Judgements

*   **Case Filed (2076 BS):** The CIAA filed a charge sheet at the Special Court, demanding the confiscation of the illegally acquired assets.
*   **Conviction (January 23, 2024):** In a judgment covering seven corruption cases, a Special Court bench comprising Chairman Teknarayan Kunwar and members Tejnarayan Singh Rai and Ritendra Thapa sentenced Madan Prasad Gupta to **five years in prison**.
*   **Fine and Penalty:** In one of the cases, he was ordered to pay a fine of **NPR 4,754,000** and an additional **NPR 9,508,000** as compensation for the embezzled revenue funds.
