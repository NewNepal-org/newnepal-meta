## **Case Research: Nepal Airlines - China Southwest Aircraft Lease**

### **1. Case Summary**

This case concerns allegations of corruption and financial irregularity within the Nepal Airlines Corporation (NAC) related to an aircraft lease agreement with China Southwest Airlines. The incident, referred to as the "China Southwest Scandal" (चाइना साउथवेष्ट काण्ड), reportedly occurred around the Nepali year 2055 (1998-1999 AD). Allegations suggest the deal resulted in a significant financial loss for the state-owned airline.

### **3. Description**

<p>The case involves an aircraft rental agreement between Nepal Airlines Corporation (NAC) and China Southwest Airlines around 1998-1999. This period was marked by several corruption scandals at the national airline. The Nepali Wikipedia page for Nepal Airlines lists the "China Southwest Scandal" (चाइना साउथवेष्ट काण्ड) as one of the significant corruption cases that plagued the airline, alongside others like the Dhamija, Chase Air, and Lauda Air scandals.</p><p>According to the initial information provided for this research, the deal led to a financial loss of approximately <strong>22 crore Nepali Rupees</strong>. The main individual accused in the matter was identified as <strong>BK Man Singh</strong>. At the time of the alleged incident, <strong>Bhim Rawal</strong> served as the Minister of Culture, Tourism, and Civil Aviation from 1998 to 1999, placing him in a position of authority over the airline during the period in question.</p><p>Despite the scandal being listed in public records, detailed online documentation, news reports from the era, and investigation outcomes by authorities like the Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority (CIAA) were not available in the conducted searches. The case is distinct from the more recent and widely documented issues concerning Chinese-made MA60 and Y12 aircraft that Nepal Airlines acquired in the 2010s.</p>
