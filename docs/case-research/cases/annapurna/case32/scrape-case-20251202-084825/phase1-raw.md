Here are the raw findings regarding the "Carpet Scandal" of 2034 BS (1977-78 AD).

### Case Details
*   **Case Name:** Carpet Scandal (गलैचा काण्ड), also referred to as 'carpet scam' and 'garment scandal'.
*   **Year:** The case was filed in 1978 AD. The initial document notes the year 2034 BS, which corresponds to 1977-1978 AD. Investigations may have begun as early as 1976 AD.
*   **Location:** Nepal.
*   **Investigating Body:** Commission for Control of Abuse of Authority (अख्तियार दुरुपयोग निवारण आयोग), which was formed in 1975. The carpet scandal was its first major case.

### Individuals and Entities Involved
*   **Main Accused:** Dr. Tulsi Giri (Then-Prime Minister of Nepal).
*   **Associated Individuals:** A total of 91 individuals, described as "bigwigs including politicians, businessmen, and bureaucrats," were implicated. Another source mentions that 125 persons were investigated, specifically naming then-Finance Minister Dr. Prakash Chandra Lohani alongside Dr. Giri.

### Allegations and Facts
*   **Nature of Scandal:** The case concerned corruption in the carpet/garment industry. One source specifies it as an "overinvoicing carpet scandal". Another refers to it as a "garment scandal".
*   **Giri's Stance:** Dr. Giri publicly stated that he was a victim of conspiracy and was "ready to be hanged if corruption charges against him were proven".

### Timeline of Events
*   **1975:** The Commission for Control of Abuse of Authority was established to control high-level corruption.
*   **December 1, 1975 - September 12, 1977:** Dr. Tulsi Giri served as the Prime Minister of Nepal.
*   **1976:** According to one academic source, the Corruption Control Commission investigated 125 individuals, including Prime Minister Dr. Tulsi Giri, in relation to the scandal.
*   **September 9, 1977:** Dr. Giri's cabinet was unexpectedly dissolved by a palace notice while he was at Singha Durbar.
*   **1978:** The Commission for Control of Abuse of Authority formally charged Dr. Giri in the 'carpet scam'.
*   **1979:** Despite the charges, Dr. Giri was brought back into active politics by conservative Panchas for the 1979 referendum campaign.
*   **May 14, 1980:** Following the referendum results, Dr. Giri announced his "political retirement".

### Outcome
*   **Acquittal Claim:** One source, citing a 2003 publication, states that the accused, including Dr. Giri, were granted bail shortly after being charged and were found "free from guilt".
